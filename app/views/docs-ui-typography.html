Typography

Good typography adds personality to an application, and can help set tone in the absence of visual elements. Tink uses Lato, a sans serif font typeface created by Łukasz Dziedzic (“Lato” means “Summer” in Polish). He wanted to create a typeface that would seem quite transparant when used in body text, but would display some original traits when used in larger sizes. The semi-rounded details of the letters give Lato a feeling of warmth, while the strong structure provides stability and seriousness.

Tinker Bell (Tink for short), is a fictional character from J. M. Barrie's 1904 play Peter Pan and its 1911 novelization Peter and Wendy. She has appeared in multiple film and television adaptations of the Peter Pan stories, in particular the 1953 animated Walt Disney picture Peter Pan. She also appears in the official sequel Peter Pan in Scarlet by Geraldine McCaughrean commissioned by Great Ormond Street Hospital as well as the "Peter and the Starcatchers" book series by Ridley Pearson and Dave Barry. At first only a supporting character described by her creator as "a common fairy", her animated incarnation was a hit and has since become a widely recognised unofficial mascot of The Walt Disney Company, and the centrepiece of its Disney Fairies media franchise including the direct-to-DVD film series Tinker Bell.
